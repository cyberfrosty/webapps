Artist Statement

I am exploring the narrative I have about my disabilities due to work related injuries and surgeries and what my arms meant to me when I was fully able-bodied. In my first piece, I focused on a period in my life when I had to undergo multiple surgeries to my upper extremities. The assault and pain I experienced is evident in the dismembered arms floating in the pool of water. At the same time, I show optimism and recovery in the ladder leading up to a sunny meadow. In four years of hiking the trails above my house, I never saw any rattlesnakes. In September, I encountered three rattlesnakes and I was having dreams about evil snakes. Because snakes shed their skin as they grow, they are symbolic of rebirth, transformation, and healing. But it is snakes’ characteristic that they must stop moving when they kill and ingest their food that made me realize that they are a metaphor for an important lesson I learned when I injured my arms - I need to stop and rest and take a break from activities and not just keep toiling away at them. In my third piece, a snake is seen with an arm that is cut open for reconstructive surgery. Here the snake symbolizes improved health and the evil of a system of workers’ compensation that pushes injured workers into surgery. My final print, Exam Room #3, shows the arm being attended to by several snakes in the examination room.
